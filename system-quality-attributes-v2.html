Service Levels · 01

SLA · SLO · SLI

SLI measures reality. SLO is the internal target. SLA is the customer-facing contract. When SLI drops below SLO, you risk breaching the SLA.

🔗 HOW ALL METRICS FLOW INTO SLA COMPLIANCE
High Availability
+ Low MTTR
+ Fault Tolerance
→
SLI (measured)
stays high
e.g. 99.97%
→
SLI > SLO
target of 99.95%
✓ Buffer maintained
→
SLO > SLA of 99.9%
✓ Contract met
No penalties
⚠ Any degradation in the chain above (slow response time, downtime, low throughput) → SLI drops → error budget burns → SLA breach risk
📜 SLA — Legal contract with customer. "99.9% uptime or you get service credits." External. Enforceable.
🎯 SLO — Internal target. Must be stricter than SLA to have a buffer. "We target 99.95% internally." Team accountability.
📊 SLI — What's actually measured. "This month we achieved 99.97%." Real data from monitoring.
